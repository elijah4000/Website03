The Himalayas

The Himalayas are a massive mountain strething all across Asia home to the tallest and most dangerous mountains in the world. Occupying an area of over five hundred thousand square kilometres across six differents countries it is home over 3 thousand mountains the tallest of which being mount Everest.

The Alps

The Alps are the highest and most extensive mountain range system in Europe, stretching approximately 1,200 kilometers across eight countries, including France, Switzerland, and Italy. The Alps are home to iconic peaks like Mont Blanc and the Matterhorn. This majestic range is not only a natural barrier but also a source of major European rivers, such as the Rhône and the Rhine

The Rockies

The Rocky Mountains, often referred to as the Rockies, are a major mountain range in western North America, stretching over 4,800 kilometres from northern British Columbia in Canada to New Mexico in the United States. The Rockies are known for their dramatic peaks and valleys sculpted by tectonic activity and glacial erosion. This vast range is home to numerous national parks and is a popular destination for outdoor activities such as hiking, skiing, and mountaineering

The Andes

The Andes Mountains, or the Andean Mountain Range, are the longest continental mountain range in the world, stretching approximately 8,900 kilometers along the western edge of South America12. This majestic range spans seven countries: Venezuela, Colombia, Ecuador, Peru, Bolivia, Chile, and Argentina2. The Andes are home to some of the highest peaks outside of Asia, including Mount Aconcagua, which rises to 6,961 meters above sea level
